Publications

'Performance Analysis of Machine Learning Algorithms for Prediction of Brain Stroke'

COMM-MANAGE Conference Book, 2023


Brain stroke remains a leading cause of death and disability worldwide, emphasizing the critical importance of early detection and timely treatment for improving patient outcomes. Machine learning (ML) algorithms have emerged as promising tools for predicting stroke risk, but their effectiveness can vary depending on the specific algorithm and dataset employed. In our study, we evaluated the performance of various ML algorithms for predicting stroke risk using a comprehensive dataset of patient data. Our findings revealed that the Random Forest algorithm outperformed its counterparts, achieving the highest accuracy. XGBoost, KNN, and Decision Tree followed closely behind. This study underscores the potential of ML algorithms in stroke risk prediction, highlighting the importance of selecting the right algorithm for specific datasets to optimize outcomes.
